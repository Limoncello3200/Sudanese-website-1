Baladi is a filling round bread eaten all across North Africa and the Middle East in some form or another, most famously called Pita in the Levant. Hollow on the inside, these breads are perfect for scooping up stews and are eaten with most meals.
  • Ingredients
    • 500g whole weat four
    • 2 tsp dried yeast
    • 2 tbs salt
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 300-400ml lukewarm water
  • Directions
    1. In a large bowl mix together: flour, salt and water. Mix and knead into a dough ball into the consistency of a shaggy dough.
    2. In a separate smaller bowl mix together: yeast, sugar and 200-300ml water and allow the yeast to rise (about 10 minutes).
    3. Pour the yeast mixture into the dough and mix together, kneading until a smooth dough is formed.
    4. Make individual circular-shaped breads and flatten to a thickness of 2-3cm.
    5. Lightly coat both sides with sesame oil, and coat the bottom side with coarse semolina before placing in the oven to stop it sticking
    6. Bake in the oven at 180C for approx. 30mins or until risen and golden brown.
