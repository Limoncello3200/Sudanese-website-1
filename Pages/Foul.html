Foul is a staple breakfast in Sudan, and each household has a different recipe. This is the recipe I use!
  • Ingredients
    • 2 300g tins ofd fava beans, drained and rinsed
    • 150 ml boiling water
    • 1 tbs ground cumin
    • 1 tsp ground coriander
    • 2 tsp salt
    • 1 tsp mixed spice
    • 1 medium red onion, diced
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 medium tomatoes, diced/li>
    • 100 ml sesame oil (50 ml to serve)
    • 50 g salty sudanese cheese, or feta, to serve
    • 1 lime
  • Directions
    1. Heat the oil in a medium sized pot on low to medium heat, then add the onion and fry, semi-covered and stirring occasionally, for 6 to 8 minutes until the pieces soften and begin to golden.
    2. Add the tomato and fry for 5 minutes until it softens and breaks down, then the colour changes into a deep red.
    3. Stir in the rinsed fava beans and water. Season with the spices and salt. Bring to a gentle boil then lower to a medium to low heat for 5 to 10 minutes to allow the beans to cook and release their juices.
    4. Add the juice from half a lime. Taste and balance the flavours by adding more of any of the seasonings. Serve in a deep round bowl and crumble the salty cheese on top, sprinkle a dash of cumin, and finally drizzle the remaining sesame oil over the top. Serve with baladi and foul.
