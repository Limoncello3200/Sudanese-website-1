Kisra, a delicate sorghum flatbread, offers a slightly tangy flavor and tender texture, perfect for scooping up every last drop of mullah ahmar, or another stew of your choice.
  • Ingredients
    • 475g sorghum flour
    • 350 ml water
    • 250g wholewheat flour
    • 30ml sesame oil
  • Directions
    1. Mix the sorghum flour with the water and let sit overnight (at least 12 hours).
    2. Stir in the wholegrain flour and enough to form a thin batter (around).
    3. Ladle the batter on a skillet lightly brushed with oil, and tilt the pan so the batter spreads around to form a thin uniform layer.
    4. Cook on medium heat until the edges of the kisra begin to curl up
